AUGUST, HONESTLY

Expect 30–32°C days, ~23°C nights, humid, with a real chance of a late-day thunderstorm (~12 rain days). The itinerary is built for it: outdoors 7–11 AM, air-conditioning and naps 1–4 PM, golden hour and nights after. Pack sun hats, folding umbrellas (rain and shade), sunscreen, electrolytes. Storms pass in an hour — shelter, tea, carry on.

DAY 02

Self-Drive: Badaling & the Yanqing Loop

MONDAY, AUGUST 3 · CAR DELIVERED 06:30 · HOME ~21:30
驾
Your own wheels: the monumental Wall at opening, a tofu-feast village, Beijing's best driving road, and copper-pot lamb on the way home.

The shape of the day: this is exactly how a Beijing family with a car does the Wall — hit Badaling at opening, then turn your back on the tour buses and spend the afternoon in Yanqing's orchard-and-canyon country, eating where the locals drive to eat. ~290 km round trip, all expressway and scenic two-lane.  ▸ About Badaling

THE WHEELS — RENTAL LOGISTICS

Book 7–10 days ahead (step-by-step in brief item 1): eHi 一嗨租车 (English app/site; China's door-to-door rental pioneer) — a Buick GL8 or similar 7-seat MPV, ~¥450–600/day plus ~¥80/day full damage waiver (不计免赔 — take it). Alternates: CAR Inc 神州租车, or Trip.com's rental aggregator.

Delivery: request 送车上门 (door delivery) to 工人体育场北路21号楼 at 06:30, Mon Aug 3 (fee ~¥50–100) and 上门取车 (door collection) Tue 07:00. An agent meets you curbside with the contract — photograph every panel, wheels, windscreen and the fuel gauge before signing.

Documents: the driver's Chinese licence + passport; a credit card for the deposit (~¥3,000–4,000 pre-auth, plus a ~¥2,000 violation hold released ~30 days after return). Only the registered renter may drive.

The detail that matters: Beijing bans one pair of plate tail-numbers inside the 5th Ring 07:00–20:00 each weekday. When booking, tell eHi the car must be street-legal Monday (周一不限行) — a routine request they handle daily. Enter the plate in Amap and it self-checks.

Return: Plan A (easiest) — overnight the car in a lot near Building 21 (~¥40–80) and hand keys to the eHi agent at 07:00 Tue as you leave for the palace. Plan B — drop tonight at the 24-h eHi/CAR branch at PEK T3 (40 min from Yangfang, straight up the airport expressway) and take two cabs home (25 min, ~¥100). Decide when booking.

P1/P2 are the big surface lots directly in front of the ticket centre (~¥10/h; ~5-min walk to the gates). Aim for zones F–J — nearest the west cable-car station. Passports + reservation QR at the gate (booked, brief item 2); cable car round trip ¥140.

08:40

Three hours on the ramparts

Cable car to the N7 platform, then climb to North Tower 8 (北八楼, 888 m) — the “Hero Slope” summit with the classic dragon-spine view. Push on to N10–N12, where the pack thins dramatically and the wall dives and rears at its steepest. Vendors sell cold water (¥10); the steps are irregular by design — grippy shoes.

QUIETER STILL  If the North side feels busy, the South section (S1–S7) across the gate is the same wall at one-third the density

Ten minutes out of the tourist gravity, into orchard country — 28 km of easy two-lane to Liugou village, ~35 min. This is where the day stops being an itinerary and starts being a road trip.

12:10

The Tofu Feast — Liugou village 柳沟豆腐宴

Beijing families drive an hour for this and nothing else: a small walled “tofu village” whose farmhouse courtyards all serve 火盆锅豆腐宴 — a charcoal fire-basin bubbling with three-colour tofu, cabbage and crackling pork belly, ringed by twenty village side dishes. Park at the village lot, pick any courtyard with cars outside (乡里乡亲 and 观澜别院 are reliable names), ~¥50–68 pp for the set feast. You will likely be the only foreign table in the village.

THE PASS BENEATH THE WALL · 关沟

Badaling guards the Guan'gou pass — the invasion corridor every northern army coveted, which is why the Ming built their showcase wall here, “the lock on the north gate of Beijing.” The valley you'll drive through after lunch was the quiet granary behind that lock: garrison towns like Yongning fed the wall's soldiers, and their courtyard kitchens still feed whoever comes over the pass.

THE NEIGHBOURHOOD · FACTORY 798 大山子

Built by East German engineers in the 1950s to make electronics for the socialist bloc, Factory 798's Bauhaus halls were saved from the wrecking ball by artists who squatted them in 2002. The slogans on the beams are original; the art beneath them changes monthly — the whole district is Beijing's shrug at anyone who says it only does ancient.

The Modern City

SWAPS, ADD-ONS & RAIN PLANS
潮
Young-Beijing picks — every one fine for the 15-year-old, zero bar streets.

You asked for modern over operatic — the acrobatics night, the self-drive canyon day and 798 are already yours. If energy or weather opens a slot, these are the city's best 15-to-35 plays right now, all within easy reach of Sanlitun. Each name links to a source worth reading.

Pop Land — POP MART City Park 泡泡玛特城市乐园

The toy giant's park inside Chaoyang Park, 10 minutes from your door: life-size Labubu, Molly and Dimoo lands, character shows, and the mother of all blind-box shops (park-exclusive editions). Freshly upgraded for 2026; half a day is plenty. Pairs naturally with a lakeside stroll in Chaoyang Park after.

teamLab Massless Beijing 无相艺术空间

China's largest immersive digital-art space — nearly 10,000 m² of light oceans, mirrored infinity rooms and interactive projection at 11-metre ceiling height. The single best thunderstorm insurance in the city, and the group photo factory of the trip. Booking: timed-entry slots on Trip.com or Damai, 1–2 days ahead in summer; go early or late to dodge school groups.

Liangma River night cruise 亮马河游船

The 45-minute cruise Beijingers nicknamed “the Seine of Beijing” — fountains, glowing bridges, embassy-district waterfront, all G-rated, from Yansha Pier 8 minutes off your door. Booking: Trip.com, a day ahead for summer evening slots (¥100–150). The riverside boardwalk is free if the boats are full — it bumped off Day 1 for the foot massage, but it slots into any spare evening.

Olympic Green by night 鸟巢 · 水立方

The 2008-and-2022 icons at their best hour: the Bird's Nest glowing ember-red, the Water Cube rippling through blues, LED kites over the vast axis plaza. Free to roam, 25 minutes by cab — the classic post-dinner add-on any clear evening (it lost its Day 2 slot to the road trip; reclaim it if you have the legs).

A luxury department store staged as a Mars colony — breathing animatronic sheep, a robotic “future farm,” space-station corridors between the sneakers. Free, air-conditioned, 15 minutes south; the most photographed retail floor in China. Go for the spectacle, not the price tags.
